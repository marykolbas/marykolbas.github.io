Welcome!

I'm Mary Kolbas, a junior at Cornell University majoring in Information Science and minoring in Operations Research. I'm also a proud scholar of the Milstein Program in Technology and Humanity.

My interests revolve around the social impact of technology and how rapidly changing technologies can be effectively utilized in the innovation process to generate sustainable, community-driven solutions. I am especially driven to understand the future impact of emerging technologies on education, public awareness, and the way individuals interact with and treat each other, both online and in the real-world.

I'm always excited to talk about game theory, networks, UX, and systems optimization. I also enjoy playing cello, fencing, and calligraphy/lettering.

INFO 2300 (Intermediate Design and Programming for the Web) | Spring 2022

Playful Plants

For the INFO 2300 final project, I created a website for The Playful Plants Project (client) from scratch in PHP. This dynamic, server-side rendered website queries a database using SQL and supports sticky forms, file uploads, functional login/logout, sessions and cookies, filtering and sorting, and adding, editing, and deleting entries in the database. This project involved multiple user personas including cognitive style facets, cognitive walkthroughs, sketch itterations, and more.

AS 3111 (Milstein Sophomore Seminar) | Spring 2022

Staying Sane At Cornell: Introductory Classes

This semester long project was inspired by the promp "Staying Sane At Cornell". My group of 5 focused on the stress surrounding introductory classes, especially due to their large class size, dense material, differences in student's background experience, and the disconnect between professors and students. This exploratory project involved ethnographic interviews, creating frameworks and prototypes, and multiple itterations.

We presented our findings to a panel of Cornell administrators, including the Cornell Vice Provost for Undergraduate Education.
