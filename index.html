Milstein Summer Session | Summer 2022

FloodNet Transparency Project

FloodNet, a community and government partnership that aims to better understand flooding in New York City, tasked our team with improving transparency and public education about the flood sensors located in their neighborhoods. Given New York City's past with government surveillance and police abuse, the main goal was to educate the public that the sensors are not cameras, but rather use sound waves to calculate rising floodwaters. We created a 3D animation and Web-AR prototype to be accessible via QR-code at select sensors in high-traffic areas to provide transparency about this technology.

I was responsible for the creation of the 3D models and textures in Blender, camera work within Blender, and scripting of the final animation.
