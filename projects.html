Milstein Summer Session | Summer 2022

FloodNet Transparency Project

FloodNet, a community and government partnership that aims to better understand flooding in New York City, tasked our team with improving transparency and public education about the flood sensors located in their neighborhoods. Given New York City's past with government surveillance and police abuse, the main goal was to educate the public that the sensors are not cameras, but rather use sound waves to calculate rising floodwaters. We created a 3D animation and Web-AR prototype to be accessible via QR-code at select sensors in high-traffic areas to provide transparency about this technology.

I was responsible for the creation of the 3D models and textures in Blender, camera work within Blender, and scripting of the final animation.

Intermediate Design and Programming for the Web (INFO 2300) | Spring 2022

Playful Plants

For the INFO 2300 final project, I created a website for The Playful Plants Project (client) from scratch in PHP. This dynamic, server-side rendered website queries a database using SQL and supports sticky forms, file uploads, functional login/logout, sessions and cookies, filtering and sorting, and adding, editing, and deleting entries in the database. Milstein Sophomore Seminar (AS 3111) | Spring 2022

Staying Sane At Cornell: Introductory Classes

This semester long project was inspired by the prompt "Staying Sane At Cornell". My group of 5 focused on the stress surrounding introductory classes, especially due to their large class size, dense material, differences in student's background experience, and the disconnect between professors and students. This exploratory project involved ethnographic interviews, creating frameworks and prototypes, and multiple itterations.

We presented our findings to a panel of Cornell administrators, including the Cornell Vice Provost for Undergraduate Education.
